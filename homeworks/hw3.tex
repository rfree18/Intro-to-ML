\paragraph{1.} Suppose we are given a set of input-output pairs $D=\left\{
    (\vx_1, y^*_1), \ldots, (\vx_N, y^*_N)
\right\}$, and we want to find the best classifier among the following
hypothesis sets:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $H_{\text{perceptron}}$: Perceptron classifier
    \item $H_{\text{logreg}}$: Logistic regression
    \item $H_{\text{svm}, C}$: Support vector machine with a regularization
        coefficient $C$
\end{enumerate}
where $C$ is one of $\left\{ c_0, \ldots, c_M \right\}$. Our goal is two folds;
(1) choose the best classifier, and (2) report its generalization performance
(or its estimate). Describe in words how these two goals are met using the
nested $K$-fold cross validation.

\begin{align*}

Derive a learning rule step-by-step for each column vector $\vw_c$ of the weight
